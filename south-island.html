South Island Destinations

Our must visit destinations of New Zealand's South Island

Queenstown is one of New Zealand’s most exciting destinations. Surrounded by towering mountains, positioned on the edge of a lake, Queenstown is a hub of adventure. Whether you like to indulge your inner explorer or simply relax and enjoy the finer things in life, you'll be able to craft your perfect itinerary from Queenstown, the perfect base for exploring the area.


Kaikōura’s special talent is marine mammal encounters – whales, fur seals and dolphins live permanently in the coastal waters. Whale watching trips leave the town several times a day and the local seal colony is always entertaining. There are plenty of cafés, restaurants and shops to explore. An easy two-hour drive north of Christchurch, Kaikōura makes for a great day trip or a fun stop on your way to Marlborough.

The regions of Christchurch and Canterbury are set against the majestic backdrops of both alps and ocean. From lush vineyards and wild coastlines to sky-piercing mountains and pristine glacial lakes, Canterbury is a region of remarkable contrasts and a haven for those seeking incredible scenery and adventure. Turquoise lakes, powerful glaciers and the sky-piercing alps of the Mackenzie district and Aoraki/Mount Cook National Park.

Nelson Tasman is renowned for its stunning natural landscapes, with diverse geography capturing everything from long golden beaches to untouched forests and rugged mountains. Perhaps it’s the sun, perhaps it’s the location, but Nelson Tasman has long been a magnet for creative people. With one of the largest numbers of working artists and galleries in the country.

Dunedin is a region of unique landscapes and fascinating cultural history. Enjoy getting close to rare wildlife and soaking up the quirky city vibe. Known as the Edinburgh of New Zealand, Dunedin wears its Scottish heritage with pride. Surrounded by dramatic hills and at the foot of a long, picturesque harbour, Dunedin is one of the best-preserved Victorian and Edwardian cities in the Southern Hemisphere.

The sunny town of Blenheim is an ideal base for those who want to explore the local wine and food culture. Bike around the vineyards or catch a tour. Situated in the heart of wine-growing Marlborough it is one of the sunniest towns in New Zealand. In and around Blenheim you’ll find an array of cafes, restaurants, wineries, bars, shops, artisan food outlets and golf courses along with popular walkways along the Taylor River Reserve.

Our Top Picks Queenstown

Food & Drink

The Bunker

The Bunker has it all from the fine dining menu specialising in game and red meats to the Upstairs “Old School Cool” Cocktail Bar.

The Grille

Notably one Queenstowns the premier dining spots, enter the New York bistro-esque restaurant and you’ll be pleasantly welcomed by the stylish interior.

Blue Kanu

The contemporary restaurant interior blends Pacifica and Asian cultures together in an uber-cool and classy way—aptly named, “Polynasia”

Accommodation

Azur Lodge

Villas just five minutes drive from Queenstown, but right on the shores of Lake Wakatipu, with views across the lake to The Remarkables.

Matakauri Lodge

On the shores of Lake Wakatipu villas and suites have private balconies or decks from which to take full advantage of the stunning views.

QT Queenstown

Some of the most incredible views of the lake and the mountains in town. The bar, restaurant and best rooms have walls of glass.

Attractions

Nevis Bungy

The infamous. Complete with a 134m nail-biter of a Bungy and one of the World’s Biggest Swings, this is a rush you need to try.

Fiordland National Park

In the southwest of the South Island known for the glacier-carved fiords of Doubtful and Milford sounds.

The Remarkables

Located on Lake Wakatipu’s southeastern shores, the aptly-named Remarkables mountain range a prime destination for skiers and snowboarders.

Our Top Picks Kaikōura

Food & Drink

The Whaler

This is the place to down a range of tap beers, eat a generously sized meal and listen to some cool jukebox tunes while you’re revelling in the fun pub atmosphere.

The Pier Hotel

Seafood is where this restaurant thrives — if you want to try some of Kaikoura’s famous crayfish, this would be the place to do it!

Koura Bay Lodge

An absolute treat for those who relish the fine dining experience. Menu offerings change on a daily basis, with what produce is available at the time.

Accommodation

Manakau Lodge

Overlooking snow-dusted peaks, a view that’s nothing short of epic. There are just four luxurious guest rooms here, so you’ve almost got the idyllic setting to yourself.

Hapuku Lodge

Lodge rooms contain soaking tubs and super comfy beds, and tree houses soar 10m above the ground, giving views across olive orchards to the beach and mountains.

Fyffe Country Lodge

Mere meters from a sandy beach and lapped by the waters of Gooch Bay, Fyffe Country Lodge is full of thoughtful touches.

Attractions

Whale Watch

As New Zealand’s go to destination for all things marine life, visitors have a 95% chance of spotting giant sperm whales and everything in between.

Kaikoura Peninsula

Kaikoura Peninsula Walkway is walk that’s just fantastic. The entire track is 11km, suitable for all ages and takes roughly three hours to complete.

Fishing Charters

Kaikoura’s known as one of New Zealand’s best destinations for fishing, great if you’re a seasoned expert, or just like the sound of catching your own meal.

Our Top Picks Christchurch

Food & Drink

Inati

Don’t go to Inati for a rushed dinner. Take your time, book the chef’s table and have him explain to you exactly what each dish in his ‘Trust us’ menu contains.

The Birdwood

A family friendly venue serving contemporary Italian food, including excellent woodfired pizza and fresh, handmade pasta.

OGB

The bar food and cocktails are great but you can also opt for the dining room with a menu boasting pretty-as-a-picture dishes.

Accommodation

The George

A 53 room boutique hotel in the heart of Christchurch. Here you’ll enjoy top facilities and great views, both inside and out.

The Classic Villa

Bright pink boutique B&B converted from a Canterbury villa. Rooms have all the mod cons and there’s a delicious, Mediterranean style breakfast on offer.

Eco Villa

Housed in a large and charming villa in the centre of Christchurch. Eco Villa is a sustainable B&B which offers large, comfortable rooms.

Attractions

Botanic Gardens

A prominent central-city attraction since 1863, it boasts an array of flowering gardens, native trees, and New Zealand blooms.

TranzAlpine Train

Travelling from Christchurch to Greymouth, this is a definite must-do for those who want to soak up the South Island’s stunning alpine landscapes.

Franz Josef Glacier

Franz Josef Glacier is a 12 km long temperate maritime glacier in Westland Tai Poutini National Park located a 5 hour drive from the city.

Our Top Picks Nelson

Food & Drink

East Street

East Street Vegetarian Café could be the most chilled-out hang-out spot in Nelson, a stage where singer-songwriters perform with a large garden out back.

Harry's Bar

An inviting place that boasts an extensive cocktail menu and a boisterous atmosphere, with a menu of creative Asian-inspired dishes.

The Styx

A double-decker restaurant and bar specialising in fresh seafood. The Styx’s two dining rooms and large outdoor terrace all afford stunning views over Tasman Bay.

Accommodation

Cathedral Inn

19th century mansion turned B&B in the centre of Nelson. Rooms are individually decorated in traditional style, relax in their tree-filled garden.

Wakefield Quay House

Luxury B&B in 1905 historic villa with glorious views over Tasman Bay. Two tasteful rooms with antiques and original artworks.

Retiro Park Lodge

A charming little lodge set in 90 acres of picturesque and rugged farmland with miniature ponies and alpacas dotted across it.

Attractions

Abel Tasman

The Abel Tasman Coast Track, a long trail winding over beaches and across ridges between Marahau in the south and Wainui in the north, prepare well for these hikes!

Skydive

Take to the skies to skydive where you can see both the North and South Islands of New Zealand, the landscape will take your breath away as you fly over.

Golden Bay

No trip to Golden Bay is complete without a visit to Te Waikoropupu Springs, the largest cold water springs in the Southern Hemisphere.

Our Top Picks Dunedin

Food & Drink

Ironic Café

The menu here is incredibly appealing, comprised of breakfasts, lunches and dinners. Offerings of morning and afternoon tea will also not go amiss!

Two Chefs

An upmarket bistro with a Parisian feel. Within its dark wood interior of antique furniture, dark-stained paneling and leather seating, enjoy the traditional charm of French cuisine.

Plato

A relaxed eatery occupying a lovely spot on Dunedin’s harbor front. Since opening in 2002, the restaurant has brought an uncomplicated approach to food to the area.

Accommodation

The Chamberson Hotel

There’s only one word for this boutique in a heritage-listed former warehouse in the heart of Dunedin, and that’s unmissable!

Distinction Dunedin Hotel

Impeccably stylish, contemporary city centre hotel, housed in a handsome, subtly art-deco slab of a building, which once served as the city’s chief post office.

Scenic Hotel Southern Cross

If location is key, you won’t get much closer to the heart of things, less than 500m from the centre of Dunedin’s plaza.

Attractions

Larnach Castle

New Zealand's only castle is an important and much-loved piece of Dunedin history. Built in 1871 by William Larnach, a merchant and politician born of Scottish parents.

Port Chalmers

The village offers an interesting mix of heritage attractions, cafés and galleries. The creative community in this small town mean it's full of vibrant art and culture.

Street Art Trail

Explore the ever-growing collection of vibrant, whimsical artworks from international and local artists which are dotted throughout the central city.

Our Top Picks Blenheim

Food & Drink

Brancott Estate

The spectacular Brancott Estate Cellar Door & Restaurant is one of Marlborough’s premier cellar doors with a restaurant offering majestic views over the region.

Twelve Trees

Named after the original walnut trees that lined the entrance to the winery, lunch at our Twelve Trees Restaurant is a must for visitors.

Wairau River

The menu is built around the fresh produce that is readily available in the Marlborough district. Enjoy matching superb wines at cellar door prices with lunch.

Accommodation

Bay of Many Coves

A private, tranquil ecolodge with eleven rooms and apartments. Rooms have huge decks or floor to ceiling windows, to take advantage of the fantastic views.

Marlborough B&B

The two room B&B is a charming little place right in amongst wine country, which also has a self catering cottage set on a seven acre property.

Hotel D'Urville

Located in central Blenheim, a unique Art Deco spot featuring 1920s style interiors. With a variety of individually decorated rooms, some colourful and some classical.

Attractions

Hop On Hop Off Bus

Bus tours of Blenheim are an ideal way to visit several vineyards around the countryside without having to worry about who will be the designated driver.

Flyer Steam Train

The train is more than 100 years old, and the passenger carriages transport you back to 1915, the journey to Picton takes an hour.

Omaka Aviation Centre

The Omaka Aviation Heritage Centre is a family-friendly place to learn about aviation history. It displays vintage World War I and World War II-era airplanes and artifacts.
