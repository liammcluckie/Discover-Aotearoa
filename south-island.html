North Island Destinations

Our must visit destinations of New Zealand's North Island

Situated at the southern end of the North Island, Wellington was recently named "the coolest little capital in the world". Surrounded by nature and fuelled by creative energy, Wellington is a compact city with a powerful mix of culture, history, nature and cuisine. Fuel your visit with strong coffee and world-class craft beer – Wellingtonians are masters of casual dining, with plenty of great restaurants, night markets and food trucks.


Explore the wine village of Martinborough, at the heart of the Classic New Zealand Wine Trail. Packed with colonial charm, Martinborough features over 20 wineries, most within cycling and walking distance of the quaint village square. Some of New Zealand’s best pinot noir comes from the town’s predominately family-owned vineyards.

Rotorua is known for bubbling mud pools, shooting geysers and natural hot springs, as well as showcasing our fascinating Māori culture. From crystal-clear streams and magical forests, to epic biking trails and explosive geysers, Rotorua has it all. The city offers a raft of attractions and experiences for everyone from adventure-seekers to those just looking to unwind.

The Coromandel, renowned for its pristine beaches, misty forests and laid-back vibe, is one of New Zealand's most popular holiday destinations. A binocular’s view across the gulf from Auckland, The Coromandel is everything that a big city isn’t. With a mountainous interior cloaked in native rainforest and more than 400 kilometres of dazzling white sand beaches, it is rustic, unspoiled and relaxed.

From the base of Mount Maunganui, a white sand surf beach stretches as far as the eye can see. Catch a wave or cruise the many cafes. "The Mount" is the colloquial name for Mount Maunganui, a relaxed beach town that occupies a peninsula at the southern end of Tauranga Harbour. The peninsula is actually a huge sandbar, with a sheltered bay on the inner harbour side and a magnificent surf beach on the ocean side.

Auckland is New Zealand’s most populous city and is a multi-cultural hub of food, music, arts and culture. Auckland sprawls over volcanic hills and around twin harbours, offering an exhilarating mix of natural wonders and urban adventures. Add a sunny climate, a background rhythm of Polynesian culture and a passion for outstanding food, wine and shopping, and you’re beginning to get the picture of Auckland.

Our Top Picks Wellington

Food & Drink

Boquita

The team behind Boquita are committed to bringing the best Mexican food to the people of Wellington, not to mention the margaritas!

Little Penang

Serving up authentic Malaysian cuisine, Little Penang is a hot spot for locals and visitors alike, the perfect place if you're on a budget.

Olive

Olive truly masters the art of a chic and stylish. Everything is made using fresh, seasonal and locally-sourced produce.

Accommodation

QT Wellington

A luxurious boutique hotel with plenty of creativity to share and 163 designer rooms and apartment suites.

U Residence Hotel

Boutique apartment hotel directly across from Te Papa Museum, and a short walk to the city’s best waterfront venues, cafes and bars.

CQ Hotels

Two side-by-side lodgings, the upscale Quality Hotel and the budget-friendly Comfort Hotel, both situated in a 19th-century building.

Attractions

Cable Car

The bright red Cable Car is a classic! Enjoy a ride up from the heart of Lambton Quay for the best panoramic views of the city.

Te Papa Museum

A must visit in Wellington, Discover great treasures, stories, Māori culture, art and heritage.

Mt Victoria

Nestled between a stunning harbour and protected green belt, there’s nothing quite like the view from Mount Victoria Lookout in the central city.

Our Top Picks Martinborough

Food & Drink

Cambridge Rd

Choose natural wine, beer or cider and enjoy one of their antipasti platters at one of the most central to town vineyards. Check their website for upcoming events!

Colombo Vineyard

Lounge outside on beanbags enjoying amazing wine or relax in the restaurant with fresh wood fire pizzas from the young German-Kiwi couple owners.

Luna Estate

Nested below their Eclipse vineyard sits Luna's terrace, serving gourmet dumplings, Asian inspired dishes and their ever changing tasting menu.

Accommodation

Peppers Parehua

Luxurious accommodation set within the Martinborough wine region. Rooms feature private terraces, indulgent bathrooms and simple chic style.

Martinborough Hotel

Individually designed guestrooms with eclectic and country-style furnishings. Enjoy soaking in the bathtub after a day of wine tasting.

Aylstone

With 6 luxury rooms, a concierge service and an on-site restaurant they provide more than enough to ensure a comfortable stay.

Attractions

Wine Tours

Martinborough Wine Tours offers wine and food lovers a fully guided, fantastic day or half day out sampling some beautiful Martinborough wine.

Horse Trek

Numerous trails including farm tracks across the hills with stunning views, along the South Wairarapa coastline and a river. Treks are weather dependant.

Ranui Essentials

Just south of Martinborough is the award winning lavender farm. overlooking the Wairarapa Tararua Ranges, you'll find a purple haze as you drive up Dry River Road.

Our Top Picks Rotorua

Food & Drink

Ambrosia

Located within a short stroll from Lake Rotorua with a large outdoor seating area to enjoy the picturesque vistas whilst sharing mediterranean tapas with friends.

Atticus Finch

This very popular jaunt pride themselves on catering for all dietary needs without any compromise on quality, with their evening menu emphasizing shared meals.

Abracadabra

Abracadabra operates around ‘good food, good vibes’. Set in a rustic 1940s building serving Mexican and Middle Eastern cuisines with plenty of options for children.

Accommodation

Wai Ora

Set on the shores of Lake Rotorua, this luxury hotel and spa offers spacious and comfortable rooms with contemporary decor and bright colours.

Astelia Lodge

A homely lodge set in picturesque countryside just a short distance from Rotorua. The breakfasts are an indulgent affair and the hosts attentive.

Regent of Rotorua

Luxury boutique hotel and spa set in a classic 1800's manor building at the edge of the picturesque Rotorua Lake. Boasts a pool, gym, bar and award-winning restaurant.

Attractions

Waimangu Volcanic Valley

One of the most popular places to see the seething mud and steaming silica terraces, with a series of walkways allowing visitors to view the smoke-filled craters

Rotorua Museum

Amid the tranquil Government Gardens, is the ornate Tudor-style Old Bath House building that now contains the Rotorua Museum.

Te Wairoa

Established in 1852 by a Christian missionary, it was envisioned as a model Maori village, which could also cater to the many foreign visitors arriving.

Our Top Picks The Coromandel

Food & Drink

Pepper Tree

Earning several accolades for its food over the years, and equally winning the heart of many travellers. Hearty seafood and beef mains are among its top specialties.

Coromandel Oyster Company

If you have a soft spot for fresh seafood, a trip to the Coromandel Oyster Company is a must. The venue is located on the southern end of the Coromandel township.

Salt

This laid-back venue in Whitianga that is known for its delicious meals, beautiful marina views and friendly atmosphere.

Accommodation

Slipper Island

This place is almost too good to be true. Spend a few nights in paradise at a private island in the Coromandel, that’s just 15 minutes away from Tairua.

Wairua Lodge

Relax in this rainforest retreat centrally located 12kms from the town of Whitianga. Enjoy peace and quiet and also not travel too far to explore the Coromandel.

Beachfront Resort

Stay seconds from the sand at the Beachfront Resort, you won’t even have to cross a road before you jump into the ocean! Close enough for a day trip to Cathedral Cove and Hot Water Beach.

Attractions

Cathedral Cove

Swim the snorkel trail, enjoy a guided tour and marvel at the abundance of aquatic life in the marine reserve. Cathedral Cove is one of New Zealand’s renowned natural icons.

Homegrown Food Trail

Grown here, harvested and combined into dishes that are distinctly regional. Experience the culinary essence of The Coromandel.

The Pinnacles

A stunning 3-hour walk to the base of the craggy Pinnacles summit traverses groves of nikau palms, huge rata trees, and across streams and swing-bridges.

Our Top Picks Mt Maunganui

Food & Drink

Latitude 37

Named after its latitudinal map location, serving classic New Zealand contemporary with an innovative twist. Passionate, international chefs collaborate to create fresh, seasonal menu offerings.

The Pizza Library

A unique and iconic eatery in Mount Maunganui. This quirky book-themed restaurant specialises in handcrafted, gourmet pizzas, which are all named after famous novels.

Johney’s Dumpling House

For a taste of street food, try Johney’s Dumpling House which is found inside the trendy Rising Tide Bar.

Accommodation

Trinity Wharf

If you’re looking for a picturesque, waterfront-facing hotel, this is one of the best offerings. Room options range from apartments with expansive garden views to tranquil guestrooms.

Oceanside Resort

The Oceanside Resort and Twin Towers is a beachfront accommodation that prominently sits in the midst of Mount Maunganui.

The Reef Luxury Apartments

The Reef Luxury Apartments is comprised of 22 self contained and self serviced apartments with a 2 or 3 bedroom option, all containing 2 bathrooms.

Attractions

The Mount

Don’t waste any time to soak in those views from the summit of Mt Maunganui, aka “The Mount”. Only a 30 minute hike up the extinct volcano for the most breathtaking views.

Surf Academy

At Mount Maunganui Surf Academy they teach surfing and surfing only. This means they can concentrate on teaching to the best level possible.

Kayak Tours

Moutohorā /Whale Island, 9km off the Whakatane coast is a kayaking paradise. We sail to the island by catamaran, enjoy two hours of paddling and visit the hot water beach.

Our Top Picks Auckland

Food & Drink

Masu

Masu suits anyone who loves Japanese flavours but is open to new interpretations on the theme. The black cod, grilled lamb cutlets and crayfish spicy miso tacos fly out the door.

Gemmayze Street

A vibrant, modern upscale Lebanese eatery in a fantastic heritage arcade. For anyone who loves sharing dishes packed with bold Middle Eastern flavours.

Pasture

There are only 25 seats and there's only one option: a ten-course tasting menu focusing on open fire cooking and fermenting all served by the chefs themselves.

Accommodation

Hotel DeBrett

A converted Victorian building the 25 rooms and suites have a retro feel, accompanied by an eclectic mix of modern furniture and local art.

Skycity Grand Hotel

Large and luxurious and very central. with no fewer than three restaurants, plus all the facilities you’d expect from a hotel with 300+ rooms and suites.

Stamford Plaza

The Stamford Plaza Auckland is a smart hotel right in the centre of the city. It’s big, with 286 rooms and suites, which are neutral, fairly minimal and comfortable but with incredible views.

Attractions

Waiheke Island

Waiheke Island is the most popular of Auckland's islands to visit. The island is home to art galleries, vineyards and hosts plenty of white-sand beaches.

Sky Tower

Auckland's needle-like Sky Tower is the city's most prominent landmark and, at 328 meters high, is New Zealand's highest building.

Auckland Art Gallery

The city's most impressive cultural site. Housed in an impressive French Renaissance-style structure built in 1887, the gallery is home to New Zealand's most extensive art collection.
